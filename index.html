Cyber Crime and Large-Scale Intrusions

Rationale

The development of computer systems has revolutionised our society. It has had a significant impact on almost all aspects of our daily lives including the way we do business, connect with each other and store information. Baecker (2019) claims that as with any significant innovation, new risks can emerge from it that can have the potential to cause massive political, economic and social harm.

Significance

For corporations and large institutions, the use of computing systems is vital to their success. Almost all of the products they offer are underpinned by sophisticated digital systems, designed to service millions of users and store enormous amounts of sensitive, valuable data. For well-resourced and motivated cyber criminals, they are the perfect targets. According to the research from Muncaster (2020) in the UK, cyber crime against large businesses has doubled over the last five years with a staggering 87% of businesses with 250 or more employees being impacted by cyber crime in 2019 .

Implications

As customers and consumers of these at-risk enterprises we recognise that the threat extends well beyond the cyber realm and has the potential to impact our daily lives, whether we are inconvenienced because our software is unavailable, or something more serious like our emergency services being disrupted. Throughout this website we will examine the risks, opportunities and choices we have surrounding large-scale intrusions and cyber crime.
